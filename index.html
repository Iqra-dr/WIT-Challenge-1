    Cakes

    Tasty spongy delights to commemorate any occasion

    Cake is often served as a celebratory dish on ceremonial occasions, such as weddings, anniversaries, and birthdays. There are countless cake recipes; some are bread-like, some are rich and elaborate, and many are centuries old. Cake making is no longer a complicated procedure; while at one time considerable labor went into cake making (particularly the whisking of egg foams), baking equipment and directions have been simplified so that even the most amateur of cooks may bake a cakeOur family recipes and secret ingredients will help you master the skill of baking any type of cake you'd like

    Sometimes, the same batter for cakes can be divided to make cupcakes, for example:

    Tarts

    As the name suggests, super tangy, super tartful, delicious tiny treats

    Tarts are typically free-standing with firm pastry base consisting of dough, itself made of flour, thick filling, and perpendicular sides while pies may have softer pastry, looser filling, and sloped sides, necessitating service from the pie plateTarts can be customised and serve as both sweet or savory concoctions! They can be very healthy owing to the numerous fruits that gel well with them. Such as:

  • Berries
  • Apples
  • Canteloupes

    • Pastries and rolls

    Soft and gooey, but could be spongy too. Mouth watering delicious treats. Pastry is differentiated from bread by having a higher fat content, which contributes to a flaky or crumbly texture. A good pastry is light and airy and fatty, but firm enough to support the weight of the filling. When making a shortcrust pastry, care must be taken to blend the fat and flour thoroughly before adding any liquid. This ensures that the flour granules are adequately coated with fat and less likely to develop gluten. On the other hand, overmixing results in long gluten strands that toughen the pastry. In other types of pastry such as Danish pastry and croissants, the characteristic flaky texture is achieved by repeatedly rolling out a dough similar to that for yeast bread, spreading it with butter, and folding it to produce many thin layers.

    Muffins

    If you're all hoity toity about health, I guess this is your closest deal to having a tasty treat. Be sure to not pick the bran ones!:)

    Muffins have a denser body than cupcakes but if baked right, can be just as sensational in taste. They are less calorically dense too, owing to the fact that they require much less sugar:
    Your "average" muffin from, say, a leading chain of café-bakeries, is 5.5 ounces and has 511 calories; its average cupcake is 3 ounces and 356 calories
